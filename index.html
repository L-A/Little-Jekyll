Create, manage and export your Jekyll sites

Little Jekyll is an app for Jekyll, the amazing static website generator (the what?). It keeps a list of your Jekyll projects, serves them in a click, and throws in live-reloading because you're a great person and you deserve it.

A static website generator is a tool that transforms content into a website. Instead of doing this all the time and for every visitor (like a server running PHP or Ruby), a generator does it before you put your files online. "Static" means that the files don't change afterwards. You update a site by changing your content, running the generator, and placing the resulting files on your server of choice.

Why Jekyll?

Jekyll is a mature generator that doesn't force your hand. You can do a ton of fun stuff with it, yet you don't have to learn more than HTML to get started. As you become familiar with Jekyll, you also acquaint yourself with other common tools like YAML, Markdown and Liquid.

Github Pages run on Jekyll, for example. They're popular, free and reliable. Other people generate their site with Jekyll and host it on Amazon's S3 service, for example.
